For the Love of Philosophy

Posted on 1/29/2021

It took a while, but here we are. If this is our first exchange -- great to have you! If not -- you know what you're getting, and I'm delighted you do.

I am not a professional writer. You can tell by the number of times I have to check TheSaurus to keep things fancy. Nevertheless, I like sharing ideas, and with the ambition of having you tag along for the journey, I will do my utmost to express my thoughts as best I can. In the process, I am also teaching myself how to program websites, which explains the relatively primitive design of this blog. Bare with me as I improve on both fronts.

So, to the more relevant question at hand: why did I start this blog? Well, for most of us, the word "philosophy" is (sadly) off-putting. We perceive it as this excessively fancy, hipster-inspired concept for those who have nothing better to do than to waste their time. My task here is to invalidate that viewpoint. "How", you ask? By showing you how often we philosophize.

You philosophize when you decide whether you should get a job or degree; try a new restaurant instead of your reliable favorite; opt for a certain political party; allow your kid to pick up boxing; ask someone to become your significant other; I could go on. Every choice you make is grounded in some set of values you have defined for yourself, which in turn set the tone for the reality you perceive. You may think that some choices are completely arbitrary: but that is either because you have thought about the same concept sometime before and saved the setting, or you're in a situation beyond your locus of control.

We are all philosophers, without really framing ourselves as such. Might as well embrace what we can't escape, right?
